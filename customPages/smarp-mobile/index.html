Smarp is the leading employee communication and advocacy platform, serving hundreds of enterprise companies. Using Smarp, businesses can create, aggregate, and automatically deliver personalized content to every employee. Over 300 companies across the globe use Smarp, including Google, Amazon, Salesforce, Marriott, Swarovski, and KPMG.

I was part of a team of three UX designers and a UX researcher. For this project, I worked within an agile team with backend and mobile developers. The business side was represented by a product owner, customer success managers, and the company management team.

Previous Version: Desktop First

The previous version just copied the UX of the Desktop application.


Should Mobile Be Different?

The main app–the desktop version–used a typical UI pattern that is used by many social media platforms. It has a Post Feed in the main area and the Channel navigation in the sidebar. As you can see, the mobile app has the same UI solutions.

Important Factor: Enterprise Customers

Smarp's main customers are companies with thousands of employees. It was important to consider their current and potential use cases during our revamp of the mobile app. 

Design Plan and User Personas

To manage all the activies, I created the project plan. Two main user personas can get value from the redesign. The first is the End User, the employee who uses the app for consuming content. The second is the Community Manager, the person who creates content.

Discovery and User Interviews

We discussed the personas and their needs internally. Then we had some interviews with representatives of each persona. Based on this information, I finalized User Personas and User Stories.

Wireframes

We had 4 internal iterations during the wireframing stage. After each iteration, we had a meeting with business stakeholders, salespeople, and developers.

Prototype

I created Figma prototypes that had all the needed functionality for user testing.

Prototype Testing

We had 2 iterations of the testing. In total, we had 9 sessions with representatives of both user personas.

Results

  1. We got positive user feedback from different channels. The feature positively affected the number of reads and mobile user’s engagement level.
  2. New channels of content delivery were created. Users can see the information in a more structured way. Content creators have more flexibility to provide and highlight different information.
  3. Updated information architecture of our mobile apps became more flexible and allowed us to add new sections and screens.
